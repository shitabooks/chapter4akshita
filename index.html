Chapter Four

Empirical and Statistical Analysis of Intellectual Property Rights Enforcement in Indian E-Commerce

Option 1: Empirical Assessment of Intellectual Property Infringement on Indian E-Commerce Platforms (2019–2025)

Nature: Quantitative and secondary data analysis

What this chapter would contain:

  • Platform-wise data on reported counterfeit listings
  • Takedown volumes
  • Repeat infringers
  • Year-wise trend analysis of IPR complaints
  • Sectoral breakdown (electronics, fashion, pharmaceuticals, books, software)
  • Graphs, tables, and percentage growth/decline trends

Data sources: Government reports (DPIIT, DIPP, MeitY), platform transparency reports, brand protection disclosures, and court records.

Why examiners like it: It converts abstract infringement debates into measurable market behaviour.

Option 2: Survey-Based Study on Awareness and Enforcement of IPR Among E-Commerce Stakeholders

Nature: Primary data, questionnaire-based research

  • Online sellers (MSMEs)
  • Consumers
  • Legal professionals
  • Brand owners

Key variables measured:

  • Awareness of trademark and copyright protection
  • Experience with counterfeit products
  • Reporting behaviour and satisfaction levels
  • Perceived effectiveness of platform grievance mechanisms

Statistical tools: Percentage analysis, Likert scale interpretation, cross-tabulation.

Why this works: It establishes the gap between law on paper and law in action.

Option 3: Statistical Analysis of IPR Litigation Arising from E-Commerce Transactions in India

Nature: Judicial data analytics

  • Number of IPR cases linked to e-commerce
  • Success rates of rights holders
  • Interim injunction versus final relief
  • Average case duration
  • Monetary damages awarded

Presentation: Year-wise tables, court-wise comparison, pre- and post-Commercial Courts Act trends.

Academic value: Transforms case law into empirical judicial trends.

Option 4: Economic Impact of Counterfeit Goods in Indian E-Commerce

Nature: Economic and policy data analysis

  • Revenue loss to legitimate brands
  • Tax revenue leakage
  • Employment impact in affected sectors
  • Consumer harm indicators

Sources: OECD reports, FICCI and ASSOCHAM studies, DPIIT and Customs seizure data.

Why it strengthens the thesis: It frames IPR enforcement as a national economic concern.

Option 5: Comparative Empirical Study of IPR Complaint Resolution Mechanisms

Nature: Platform compliance benchmarking

  • Average takedown time
  • Transparency of grievance systems
  • Repeat infringement control
  • Seller reinstatement rates

Outcome: Ranking of platforms, identification of best practices and failures.

Why this is powerful: Introduces regulatory accountability through data.
